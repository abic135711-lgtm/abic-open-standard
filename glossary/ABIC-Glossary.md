# üß≠ Governance & Ethics

### **Access Neutrality**

The principle that orbital infrastructure must be equally accessible to all signatory jurisdictions, regardless of economic, military, or political status. Enforced by **IFOC** through **Corridor Operating Certificates (COC)** and non-discriminatory scheduling.

### **Red-Flag Suspension**

A temporary halt of treaty privileges triggered by verified violations, pending review and remediation. Designed to prevent escalation while preserving accountability. Evaluated by the **Hazard Review & Red-Flag Board (HRRB)** with mandatory **Proportionality Review**.

*Example: If an operator fails to report a tether failure within 24 hours, a Red-Flag Suspension temporarily blocks their access to high-priority corridors until compliance is restored and safety protocols are verified.*

### **Transparency Trigger**

A threshold event‚Äîsuch as launch, docking, or resource extraction‚Äîthat automatically initiates public disclosure protocols in machine-readable format through the **Civic Data Layer (CDL)**. Creates **Public Ledger Events (PLE)** that cannot be suppressed.

*Example: When a cargo container docks at a depot, its manifest, contractor information, and beneficial owners are automatically published to the public dashboard within 60 seconds.*

### **Beneficial Ownership**

The true individuals or entities who benefit from a contract, asset, or vendor relationship, regardless of formal registration or shell structures. Required disclosure prevents covert monopolistic control.

*Example: If Company A is registered in Delaware, owned by Company B in the Cayman Islands, which is owned by Person C, then Person C is the beneficial owner‚Äîeven if only Company A's name appears on the contract. ABIC requires Person C's disclosure.*

### **Beneficial Ownership Threshold**

The percentage of effective control (typically 5‚Äì10%) at which an individual or entity must be publicly disclosed under ABIC transparency rules. Managed by the **Beneficial Ownership & Contract Integrity Office (BOCIO)**.

### **Public Stewardship**

The ethical obligation to manage infrastructure, data, and resources in service of the public good, not private gain. Core principle underlying all **Operator** responsibilities.

### **Civic Covenant**

The implicit agreement between citizens and institutions that power will be exercised transparently, equitably, and accountably. ABIC makes this covenant explicit and enforceable.

### **Proportionality Review**

A mandatory evaluation ensuring that enforcement actions are fair, non-punitive, and no more restrictive than necessary to restore safety. Conducted by **HRRB** before any **Red-Flag Suspension** becomes permanent.

### **Technical Witness Panel (TWP)**

A rotating, neutral panel of engineers and data specialists responsible for validating safety events, anomalies, and red-flag triggers. Reports to the **Independent Safety & Compliance Bureau (ISCB)**.

### **Vendor Integrity Registry**

A public database listing all contractors, subcontractors, and beneficial owners contributing to ABIC infrastructure. Maintained by **BOCIO** as part of the **Contract Ledger**.

### **Dispute-Escalation Track**

A tiered conflict-resolution pathway moving from data-first mediation ‚Üí independent review ‚Üí treaty arbitration. Final binding decisions issued by the **Treaty Arbitration Chamber (TAC)**.

### **Safety Event**

A verified incident posing immediate risk to life, infrastructure, or mission integrity. Triggers investigation by ISCB and may initiate a Red-Flag Suspension. See also: Anomaly, Remediation Plan, HRRB

### **Anomaly**

A deviation from expected telemetry, behavior, or environmental conditions requiring investigation and potential remediation. See also: Safety Event, TIC, TWP

### **Remediation Plan**

A corrective roadmap submitted by an Operator to restore compliance after a Safety Event or violation. Must be approved by ISCB and HRRB before lifting a Red-Flag Suspension. See also: Audit Trail, Proportionality Review

# üèõ Governance Bodies & Institutions

### **Interplanetary Framework Oversight Commission (IFOC)**  

Primary technocratic authority administering the ABIC Convention. Oversees compliance, audits, safety investigations, **Beneficial Ownership** disclosures, and certification processes. Coordinates all subsidiary bodies including **NRA**, **COCO**, **ISCB**, **ODCAC**, **MTLA**, **HRRB**, **BOCIO**, and **TAC**.

### **Node Registration Authority (NRA)**  

Certifies and records all ABIC-compliant **Orbital Nodes**. Manages **Node Classes** (A/B/C/D), **Transparency Trigger** data, readiness status, and **Beneficial Ownership** records for all registered nodes.

### **Corridor Operations Coordination Office (COCO)** 
 
Coordinates safe transit, scheduling, collision avoidance, and anomaly reporting throughout the **Neutral Corridor**‚Äîanalogous to space traffic control. Maintains real-time awareness of all **Approach Corridors** and **Capture Windows**.

### **Independent Safety & Compliance Bureau (ISCB)** 
 
Investigates safety events, **Red-Flag Suspensions**, telemetry anomalies, and deviations from engineering standards. May subpoena data or recommend shutdowns. Works with **Technical Witness Panel (TWP)** for technical validation.

### **Open Data & Civic Access Commission (ODCAC)**  

Maintains public data: **Civic Data Layer (CDL)**, **Beneficial Ownership** registries, **Contract Ledger**, compliance archives. Ensures machine-readable and freely accessible disclosures via **Live Dashboards**.

### **Mass-Transport Licensing Authority (MTLA)**  

Issues safety and operational licenses for **Mass Drivers**, **Momentum-Exchange Tethers (MET)**, **Mag-Tarzan** vehicles, and **Interplanetary Shipping Containers (ISC)**. Verifies **Interoperability Standards** compliance.

### **Hazard Review & Red-Flag Board (HRRB)**  

Evaluates hazards, collisions, tether failures, telemetry breaches, or environmental contamination events. Determines when **Red-Flag Suspensions** apply and conducts **Proportionality Reviews**.

### **Beneficial Ownership & Contract Integrity Office (BOCIO)**  

Verifies contractor legitimacy, detects shell-company laundering, and prevents covert monopolistic control of ABIC nodes. Maintains the **Vendor Integrity Registry** and enforces **Beneficial Ownership Thresholds**.

### **Treaty Arbitration Chamber (TAC)**  

Resolves disputes between **Signatories**, **Operators**, or vendors. Issues binding **Arbitration Awards**, all publicly disclosed through **ODCAC**.

### **Environmental Impact Zone (EIZ)**

A designated region within the Neutral Corridor requiring enhanced monitoring due to proximity to sensitive infrastructure, biological payloads, or high-risk operations. Subject to quarterly audits and continuous telemetry compliance. See also: COCO, HRRB, Safety Event

Example: A 5 km radius around an active mass driver is designated an EIZ due to debris risk, requiring continuous telemetry and quarterly audits.

# üë• Roles & Jurisdictions

### **Operator**  

Any public, private, or hybrid entity responsible for deploying, maintaining, or controlling infrastructure within the **Neutral Corridor**. Must hold a valid **Corridor Operating Certificate (COC)** and comply with **IFOC** standards regardless of nationality. Subject to **Beneficial Ownership** disclosure requirements.

### **Signatory**  

A national-level government that ratifies the **Framework Convention** and participates in **IFOC** governance and **TAC** arbitration. Bound by **Non-Derogation Principle** to maintain minimum standards.

### **Jurisdiction**  

Any subnational or administrative entity‚Äîstate, province, city, or agency‚Äîempowered to implement ABIC standards. May exercise **Jurisdictional Override** to adopt ABIC independently of national ratification.

### **Corridor Operating Certificate (COC)**  

A mandatory license granting an **Operator** permission to engage in mass-transport, docking, or node activities within the **Neutral Corridor**. Issued by **IFOC** after safety certification by **ISCB** and contractor verification by **BOCIO**.

# ‚öôÔ∏è Engineering & Infrastructure

### **Orbital Node**  

A discrete infrastructure unit‚Äîsatellite, station, depot, or tether hub‚Äîgoverned by ABIC transparency, **Interoperability Standards**, and safety protocols. Must be registered with **NRA** and assigned a **Node Class**.

*Example: A fuel depot at L2 Lagrange point would be registered as a Class B node, with public disclosure of its operator, beneficial owners, docking capacity, and real-time status.*

### **Node Class (A/B/C/D)**  

Classification system defining a node's operational capacity:
- **Class A:** Micro-relay nodes (communications, sensors)
- **Class B:** Medium nodes (refueling, cargo transfer)
- **Class C:** Major hubs (processing, manufacturing)
- **Class D:** Heavy-industrial complexes (large-scale operations)

### **Interoperability Standard**  

Ensures all ABIC systems can communicate, share data, and synchronize across jurisdictions. Includes **Docking Harmonization Standard**, data formats, and safety protocols. Certified by **MTLA**.

### **Machine-Readable Format**  

Structured data formats (XML, JSON, CSV) enabling automated transparency and civic integration through the **Civic Data Layer (CDL)**. Required for all **Public Ledger Events (PLE)**.

### **Live Dashboard**  

Real-time public interface displaying node status, contracts, vendors, **Beneficial Owners**, and compliance history. Maintained by **ODCAC** and accessible via **Open-Source Pilot** implementations.

### **Open-Source Pilot**  

A forkable reference implementation of the ABIC public dashboard, allowing any jurisdiction or watchdog group to deploy their own monitoring interface using **Civic Data Layer** feeds.

### **Neutral Corridor**  

A demilitarized, non-exclusive logistics zone across cislunar and interplanetary space guaranteeing safe passage for all **Signatories**. Managed by **COCO** with safety oversight by **ISCB**.

### **Momentum-Exchange Tether (MET)**  

A rotating tether that transfers momentum between spacecraft and cargo pods, enabling low-propellant transport. Must meet **Rotational Safety Envelope** requirements and be licensed by **MTLA**.

*Example: A 20km rotating tether catches incoming cargo pods traveling at 3 km/s and releases them toward their destination with adjusted velocity, all without using propellant‚Äîjust momentum exchange.*

### **Mass Driver (Rail Accelerator)**  

An electromagnetic launcher that accelerates standardized **Interplanetary Shipping Containers (ISC)** to rendezvous velocity. Requires **MTLA** licensing and **ISCB** safety certification.


### **Interplanetary Shipping Container (ISC)**  

A modular cargo unit meeting ABIC dimensional, thermal, and safety standards across **Mass Drivers**, **Momentum-Exchange Tethers**, and depots. Features standardized docking interfaces per **Docking Harmonization Standard**.

*Example: A 2m √ó 2m √ó 3m standardized container can be launched from an asteroid mass driver, caught by a momentum-exchange tether, transferred to a depot, and delivered to lunar orbit‚Äîall using the same interface specifications.*

### **Closed-Loop Life Support (CLLS)**  

Renewable life-support system providing air, water, and nutrient recycling for long-duration missions. Required for crewed **Orbital Nodes** and **Ark** vessels.

### **Energy Depot**  

A solar, nuclear, or hybrid node distributing power to ABIC infrastructure. Nuclear-powered depots require additional safety certifications and are subject to enhanced **ISCB** oversight.

### **Capture Window**  

The spatial-temporal window in which cargo or vessels may be safely received by tethers, depots, or **Mag-Tarzan** vehicles. Coordinated by **COCO** to prevent collisions.

### **Capture Envelope**

The allowable spatial and velocity deviation around a Capture Window within which a vessel can still be safely intercepted by a tether, depot, or Mag-Tarzan vehicle.
See also: Capture Window, Approach Vector Certification, Rotational Safety Envelope.

### **Approach Corridor**  

The designated path for incoming vessels or cargo pods approaching a **Capture Window**, specifying velocity and alignment tolerances. Violations trigger automatic alerts through **COCO**.

### **Rotational Safety Envelope**  

The clearance required around **Momentum-Exchange Tether** systems to prevent collision or capture anomalies. Defined by **ISCB** engineering standards and enforced through **COCO** traffic management.

### **Docking Harmonization Standard**  

Mechanical and algorithmic alignment protocols governing ABIC docking operations. Ensures all **ISCs** can dock at any certified **Orbital Node** regardless of manufacturer or nationality.

### **Approach Vector Certification**

A validation process ensuring incoming vessels meet velocity, timing, and alignment tolerances for safe docking or tether capture. Managed by **COCO** and required for entry into Approach Corridors and Capture Windows. See also: Rotational Safety Envelope, Docking Harmonization Standard


### **Telemetry**

The automated transmission and reception of data from remote infrastructure, used for monitoring, diagnostics, and compliance. Telemetry forms the backbone of ABIC‚Äôs transparency architecture and is subject to Telemetric Integrity Checks (TIC). See also: TIC, Anomaly, Audit Trail

### **Ark (Interplanetary Arcology Vessel)**  

A long-duration, closed-ecology arcology vessel serving as a mobile habitat, research hub, and operations anchor during extended missions to the Belt. Maintains full biodiversity, radiation shielding, and **Closed-Loop Life Support (CLLS)**.

*Example: A 500-person Ark on a 50-year journey to the asteroid belt would function as a mobile city, complete with agriculture, manufacturing, research facilities, and full life support‚Äîessentially a small biosphere in space.*

### **Mag-Tarzan (Electromagnetic Grapple Vehicle)**  

A precision EM-grapple vehicle used for capturing cargo pods, stabilizing tether interfaces, and maneuvering standardized **ISCs**. Features vectored grapple arms, RCS thrusters, and collision-avoidance telemetry. Licensed by **MTLA**.

*Example: When a cargo pod misses its capture window by 50 meters, a Mag-Tarzan vehicle uses its electromagnetic grapples to safely retrieve the pod and guide it to the correct docking port.*

# üìú Legal & Treaty Language

### **Framework Convention**  

The foundational treaty defining ABIC principles, obligations, institutions, and annexes. Establishes **IFOC** and all subsidiary bodies, defines **Signatory** obligations, and creates enforcement mechanisms including **Red-Flag Suspensions** and **TAC** arbitration.

### **Model Bill**  

A legislative template for subnational or national adoption of ABIC standards via **Jurisdictional Override**. Allows implementation without waiting for national ratification of the **Framework Convention**.

### **Jurisdictional Override**  

Allows local jurisdictions to adopt ABIC even if their national government has not ratified the treaty. Enables cities, states, or provinces to participate independently while maintaining **Access Neutrality**.

*Example: The State of California could adopt ABIC standards and participate in the Neutral Corridor even if the U.S. federal government has not yet ratified the Framework Convention.*

### **Compliance Protocol**  

Mechanism for verifying adherence via audits, peer review, and public reporting through **ISCB** and **ODCAC**. Creates **Audit Trails** for all major operations.

### **Good-Faith Deviation Clause**  

Permits temporary deviation from ABIC rules during emergencies, with mandatory transparency afterward via **Public Ledger Events**. Does not waive liability but acknowledges operational necessities.

### **Non-Derogation Principle**  

Prevents **Signatories** from lowering transparency or safety standards below ABIC minimums. Ensures a "ratchet effect" where standards can only improve, never regress.

### **Jurisdictional Autonomy**  

Clarifies which domestic legal domains remain unaffected by ABIC participation. Preserves national sovereignty while establishing international operational standards within the **Neutral Corridor**.

### **Sovereignty Clause**  

Ensures ABIC does not grant **IFOC** authority over domestic governance or military functions outside the **Neutral Corridor**. Limits treaty scope to logistics and transparency within designated space zones.

### **Node Sovereignty**  

**Orbital Nodes** remain under their owner's jurisdiction, provided they comply with ABIC **Interoperability Standards** and transparency requirements. Owners retain control but accept public oversight.

### **Arbitration Award**  

A binding decision issued by **TAC** in treaty disputes between **Signatories**, **Operators**, or regarding **Red-Flag Suspensions**. Publicly disclosed through **ODCAC** unless sensitive security information requires limited redaction.

# üìÇ Data & Transparency

### **Civic Data Layer (CDL)**  

Standardized, machine-readable data schema for all public ABIC disclosures. Enables **Live Dashboards**, **Open-Source Pilots**, and citizen monitoring. Maintained by **ODCAC**.

### **Telemetric Integrity Check (TIC)**  

Cryptographic or multi-source verification ensuring telemetry authenticity. Prevents falsified data from triggering or suppressing **Transparency Triggers** and **Public Ledger Events**.

### **Public Ledger Event (PLE)**  

A time-stamped, automatically logged compliance or anomaly event published through the **Civic Data Layer**. Cannot be deleted or modified‚Äîonly annotated with corrections and explanations.

*Example: When a cargo container is launched, a PLE is created containing: timestamp, operator ID, beneficial owners, cargo manifest, destination, and trajectory‚Äîall cryptographically signed and publicly viewable.*

### **Contract Ledger**  

A machine-readable public registry of all contracts, subcontractors, and vendors participating in ABIC deployments, including **Beneficial Ownership** disclosures. Maintained by **BOCIO** and published via **ODCAC**.

### **Audit Trail**  

A cryptographically verifiable sequence of operational and telemetry records used for transparency and compliance enforcement by **ISCB**. Required for all **Corridor Operating Certificates**.

### **Open-Access Archive**  

A public repository containing historical treaty updates, compliance reports, engineering modifications, and **Orbital Node** specifications. Enables long-term analysis, accountability, and scientific research.

### **Versioning**

The process by which ABIC glossary and treaty terms are updated, ratified, and archived. All changes must be publicly disclosed via the Open-Access Archive and tagged with version identifiers. See also: Amendment, Open-Access Archive

### **Amendment**

A formal revision to treaty language, glossary definitions, or institutional mandates. Requires review by IFOC, publication via ODCAC, and ratification by a quorum of Signatories. See also: Versioning, Framework Convention

### **Glossary Update Protocol**

The formal process by which new terms are added, revised, or retired from the ABIC Glossary. Requires:
public versioning via ODCAC
cross-referencing with existing definitions
confirmation by IFOC Documentation Office
archival in the Open-Access Archive
See also: Versioning, Amendment, Framework Convention.
