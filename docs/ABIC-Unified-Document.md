# ğŸ§­ Governance & Ethics

### **Access Neutrality**

The principle that orbital infrastructure must be equally accessible to all signatory jurisdictions, regardless of economic, military, or political status. Enforced by **IFOC** through **Corridor Operating Certificates (COC)** and non-discriminatory scheduling.

### **Red-Flag Suspension**

A temporary halt of treaty privileges triggered by verified violations, pending review and remediation. Designed to prevent escalation while preserving accountability. Evaluated by the **Hazard Review & Red-Flag Board (HRRB)** with mandatory **Proportionality Review**.

*Example: If an operator fails to report a tether failure within 24 hours, a Red-Flag Suspension temporarily blocks their access to high-priority corridors until compliance is restored and safety protocols are verified.*

### **Transparency Trigger**

A threshold eventâ€”such as launch, docking, or resource extractionâ€”that automatically initiates public disclosure protocols in machine-readable format through the **Civic Data Layer (CDL)**. Creates **Public Ledger Events (PLE)** that cannot be suppressed.

*Example: When a cargo container docks at a depot, its manifest, contractor information, and beneficial owners are automatically published to the public dashboard within 60 seconds.*

### **Beneficial Ownership**

The true individuals or entities who benefit from a contract, asset, or vendor relationship, regardless of formal registration or shell structures. Required disclosure prevents covert monopolistic control.

*Example: If Company A is registered in Delaware, owned by Company B in the Cayman Islands, which is owned by Person C, then Person C is the beneficial ownerâ€”even if only Company A's name appears on the contract. ABIC requires Person C's disclosure.*

### **Beneficial Ownership Threshold**

The percentage of effective control (typically 5â€“10%) at which an individual or entity must be publicly disclosed under ABIC transparency rules. Managed by the **Beneficial Ownership & Contract Integrity Office (BOCIO)**.

### **Public Stewardship**

The ethical obligation to manage infrastructure, data, and resources in service of the public good, not private gain. Core principle underlying all **Operator** responsibilities.

### **Civic Covenant**

The implicit agreement between citizens and institutions that power will be exercised transparently, equitably, and accountably. ABIC makes this covenant explicit and enforceable.

### **Proportionality Review**

A mandatory evaluation ensuring that enforcement actions are fair, non-punitive, and no more restrictive than necessary to restore safety. Conducted by **HRRB** before any **Red-Flag Suspension** becomes permanent.

### **Technical Witness Panel (TWP)**

A rotating, neutral panel of engineers and data specialists responsible for validating safety events, anomalies, and red-flag triggers. Reports to the **Independent Safety & Compliance Bureau (ISCB)**.

### **Vendor Integrity Registry**

A public database listing all contractors, subcontractors, and beneficial owners contributing to ABIC infrastructure. Maintained by **BOCIO** as part of the **Contract Ledger**.

### **Dispute-Escalation Track**

A tiered conflict-resolution pathway moving from data-first mediation â†’ independent review â†’ treaty arbitration. Final binding decisions issued by the **Treaty Arbitration Chamber (TAC)**.

### **Safety Event**

A verified incident posing immediate risk to life, infrastructure, or mission integrity. Triggers investigation by ISCB and may initiate a Red-Flag Suspension. See also: Anomaly, Remediation Plan, HRRB

### **Anomaly**

A deviation from expected telemetry, behavior, or environmental conditions requiring investigation and potential remediation. See also: Safety Event, TIC, TWP

### **Remediation Plan**

A corrective roadmap submitted by an Operator to restore compliance after a Safety Event or violation. Must be approved by ISCB and HRRB before lifting a Red-Flag Suspension. See also: Audit Trail, Proportionality Review

# ğŸ“œ Legal & Treaty Language

### **Framework Convention**  

The foundational treaty defining ABIC principles, obligations, institutions, and annexes. Establishes **IFOC** and all subsidiary bodies, defines **Signatory** obligations, and creates enforcement mechanisms including **Red-Flag Suspensions** and **TAC** arbitration.

### **Model Bill**  

A legislative template for subnational or national adoption of ABIC standards via **Jurisdictional Override**. Allows implementation without waiting for national ratification of the **Framework Convention**.

### **Jurisdictional Override**  

Allows local jurisdictions to adopt ABIC even if their national government has not ratified the treaty. Enables cities, states, or provinces to participate independently while maintaining **Access Neutrality**.

*Example: The State of California could adopt ABIC standards and participate in the Neutral Corridor even if the U.S. federal government has not yet ratified the Framework Convention.*

### **Compliance Protocol**  

Mechanism for verifying adherence via audits, peer review, and public reporting through **ISCB** and **ODCAC**. Creates **Audit Trails** for all major operations.

### **Good-Faith Deviation Clause**  

Permits temporary deviation from ABIC rules during emergencies, with mandatory transparency afterward via **Public Ledger Events**. Does not waive liability but acknowledges operational necessities.

### **Non-Derogation Principle**  

Prevents **Signatories** from lowering transparency or safety standards below ABIC minimums. Ensures a "ratchet effect" where standards can only improve, never regress.

### **Jurisdictional Autonomy**  

Clarifies which domestic legal domains remain unaffected by ABIC participation. Preserves national sovereignty while establishing international operational standards within the **Neutral Corridor**.

### **Sovereignty Clause**  

Ensures ABIC does not grant **IFOC** authority over domestic governance or military functions outside the **Neutral Corridor**. Limits treaty scope to logistics and transparency within designated space zones.

### **Node Sovereignty**  

**Orbital Nodes** remain under their owner's jurisdiction, provided they comply with ABIC **Interoperability Standards** and transparency requirements. Owners retain control but accept public oversight.

### **Arbitration Award**  

A binding decision issued by **TAC** in treaty disputes between **Signatories**, **Operators**, or regarding **Red-Flag Suspensions**. Publicly disclosed through **ODCAC** unless sensitive security information requires limited redaction.
