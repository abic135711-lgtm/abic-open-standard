# ✅ 1. **Near-Field Electromagnetic Grapple Explanation**

### *Why the Mag-Tarzan grapples don’t violate physics*

A common misconception is that electromagnetic grapples behave like “magnetic tractor beams.”
They don’t. They can't. And we do NOT claim that anywhere.

Here’s the real physics:

---

### ✅ **1. EM coils provide HOLDING force, not pull-in force**

Long-range magnetic attraction is extremely weak at meter-scale distances unless you have absurd currents.

So:

* **The tender does NOT magnetically pull itself toward the asteroid.**
* It uses **winches + micro-thrusters** for approach.
* The EM coil activates **only after soft-contact** (≤ 30 cm).

---

### ✅ **2. Natural asteroids are NOT magnetic enough**

Iron-nickel (Fe-Ni) meteorites *do* respond to magnetic fields, but typical C-type asteroids do not.

Therefore:

✅ Mag-Tarzan uses **steel anchor pucks**
✅ Soft-dock happens mechanically
✅ The EM field gives a **clamping force**, not long-distance attraction

---

### ✅ **3. Why use EM clamp instead of just mechanical latch?**

Because:

* Mechanical hooks can misalign
* Microgravity contact is delicate
* EM coils give **variable, controlled grip force**
* Coil clamp gives **instant emergency release**
* No moving parts = fewer failure points

---

### ✅ **4. Grip distance: 1–30 cm**

After winch pulls tender into position, the EM coil creates a temporary high-friction coupling.

This is not fiction. This is identical in principle to:

* magnetic docking plates
* magnetic grapples used in orbital debris prototypes
* industrial mag-grippers used on Earth

# ✅ 3. **Momentum Budget: Swing vs Electric Propulsion**

### *Why Mag-Tarzan is worth the trouble*

### ✅ Electric Thruster Δv cost example

Let’s assume:

* Required maneuver: **12 m/s**
* Hall thruster Isp: 1,600 s
* Tender mass: 500 kg

Propellant consumption:

`m_prop = m0 * (1 - e^( -Δv / (Isp * g) ))`

`m_prop ≈ 2.1 kg xenon`

Small? Yes.

Now scale to a year:

* 12 m/s per task
* 3 tasks/day
* 1,000 tasks/year

→ ~2,100 kg xenon/year
→ impossible for long-duration operations.

---

### ✅ Mag-Tarzan swing Δv cost example

Single swing: **0.1–0.3 m/s**

40 swings = **4–12 m/s**

Propellant cost:

* **0 kg** (except micro-corrections)
* ~0.5–2% what EP would need

* Tender stabilizes with RCS

* Approach via backup anchor
* Deploy new anchor puck

### ✅ **If anchor pulls free:**

* Load sensors detect slip
* EM coil disengages automatically
* RCS stabilizes
* Switch to next anchor
* Mark surface as unsuitable

### ✅ **If EM coil overheats:**

* Hard shutdown
* Move to mechanical clamp
* Wait for thermal cool-down
* Continue ops without EM assist

### ✅ **If release timing is off:**

* Arc-solver recomputes vector
* RCS trim corrects trajectory
* Loss is minimal (<1–2° error)
